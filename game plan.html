The Core Feeling

Think about emotions, not mechanics. Examples: Powerful? Tense? Overwhelmed? Strategic? Unstoppable? Desperate? Choose one main feeling.
If you had 10 seconds to tell someone about your game, what would you say? This becomes your north star.
This is your unique hook. What will make people say 'oh that's interesting' instead of 'another ARPG'?

Visual Identity

Dark gritty magic needs a limited palette. Too many colors dilutes the mood. Think about what emotions these colors create.
Deep purple - corruption, magic
Blood red - danger, violence
Sickly green - poison, decay
Ash gray - stone, ancient ruins
These don't have to be ARPGs. Pick games where you love HOW they look, not what they do. This helps establish your visual direction.
Click the cards that feel right. These guide every art decision you make later.

Combat & Gameplay Feel

Not what buttons do, but the sensation. Fast or deliberate? Chaotic or tactical? Powerful or desperate?
Hades has boons. Diablo has loot. Souls has stamina management. What's your thing?
This affects everything from enemy speed to skill cooldowns. Be honest about what you find fun.
Who are they? What do they look like? Why are they here? This helps you design everything from sprites to animations.

Enemies & World

Progression & Rewards

Scope Reality Check

This section keeps you honest. Every feature you add multiplies development time. Being clear about what you're NOT making is just as important as what you are making.
This is your protection against scope creep. When you want to add something later, check this list first.
